How to test moderation in growth modeling

Posted on 2024-09-03 Edited on 2024-09-02 In blog

The outcome is repeatedly measured in the moderation.

There are two papers explaining the methods behind:

  1. Curran, P. J., Bauer, D. J., & Willoughby, M. T. (2004). Testing main effects and interactions in latent curve analysis. Psychological Methods, 9(2), 220-237.

  2. Preacher, K. J., Curran, P. J., & Bauer, D. J. (2006). Computational tools for probing interactions in multiple linear regression, multilevel modeling, and latent curve analysis. Journal of Educational and Behavioral Statistics, 31(4), 437-448.

    Note: online tools mentioned in this paper could be found at: http://www.quantpsy.org/interact/index.htm

Though moderation was not a main focus in this classic paper, gender as a moderator appeared in the growth modeling.

  1. Ployhart, R. E., & Vandenberg, R. J. (2010). Longitudinal research: The theory, design, and analysis of change. Journal of management, 36(1), 94-120.
